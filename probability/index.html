Boy or Girl paradox

This is bayesian treatment of the paradox, which doesn't require speaking about samplings or any of that nonesense. Information learned: Mr Smith has two children. At least one of them is a boy and we also learn fact $T$ about it. Let's label those children $1$ and $2$.

  • $B_1$ = child 1 is a boy
  • $B_2$ = child 2 is a boy
  • $T_1$ = T holds for child 1
  • $T_2$ = T holds for child 2
  • $BT = B_1 T_1 + B_2 T_2$ = the information we learn.

Assumptions

Child 1 and 2 are indistinguishable. So we should be able to switch $1$ and $2$ in any equations.

  • $P(T_1) = P(T_2) = t$
  • $P(B_1) = P(B_2) = \frac{1}{2}$

Without any assumptions

  • $P(B_1 B_2\ |\ BT) = \frac{P(BT)P(B_1 B_2\ |\ B_1 B_2)}{P(BT)}$
  • $P(BT) = P(BT\ |\ B_1 B_2)P(B_1 B_2) + P(BT\ |\ \bar{B_1} B_2)P(\bar{B_1} B_2) + P(BT\ |\ B_1 \bar{B_2})P(B_1 \bar{B_2}) + P(BT\ |\ \bar{B_1} \bar{B_2})P(\bar{B_1} \bar{B_2})$
  • $P(BT\ |\ \bar{B_1} B_2) = P(B_1 T_1 + B_2 T_2\ |\ \bar{B_1} B_2) = P(T_2\ |\ \bar{B_1} B_2) = P(T_2) = t$
  • $P(BT\ |\ B_1 \bar{B_2}) = t$ the same
  • $P(BT\ |\ \bar{B_1} \bar{B_2}) = 0$ obviously
  • $P(B_1 T_1 + B_2 T_2\ |\ B_1 B_2) = P(T_1 + T_2\ |\ B_1 B_2)$ trivially

Case: $T$ = child is the younger of the two

  • Cannot be true for both children at the same time, so it holds that
  • $P(T_1 + T_2\ |\ B_1 B_2) = P(T_1\ |\ B_1 B_2) + P(T_2\ |\ B_1 B_2 ) = 2 t $
  • Plug everything together to arrive at $P(B_1 B_2\ |\ BT) = \frac{1}{2}$

Case: $T_1, T_2$ are independent. For example $T$ = child born on a tuesday

  • $Q(\cdot) = P(\cdot |\ B_1 B_2)$ for brevity
  • $Q(T_1 + T_2) = Q(T_1 \bar{T_2} + T_1 T_2 + T_2 \bar{T_1})$ holds always (look at venn diagram)
  • $Q(T_1 \bar{T_2} + T_1 T_2 + T_2 \bar{T_1}) = Q(T_1 \bar{T_2}) + Q(T_1 T_2) + Q(T_2 \bar{T_1})$ because mutually exclusive (venn)
  • $Q(T_1 \bar{T_2}) = Q(T_1) Q(\bar{T_2}) = t \cdot (1-t)$ independence
  • $Q(T_2 \bar{T_1}) = Q(T_2) Q(\bar{T_1}) = t \cdot (1-t)$ independence
  • $Q(T_1 T_2) = t^2$ independence
  • Plug everything together to arrive at $P(B_1 B_2\ |\ BT) = \frac{2 - t}{4 - t}$
