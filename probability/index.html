Boy or Girl paradox

Information learned: $BT$

  • $B_1$ = child 1 is a boy
  • $B_2$ = child 2 is a boy
  • $T_1$ = child 1 is born on a tuesday
  • $T_2$ = child 2 is born on a tuesday

Assumptions

Child 1 and 2 are indistinguishable:

  • $P(T_1) = P(T_2)$
  • $P(B_1) = P(B_2) = b$
The additional information $T$ is independent of the gender:
  • $P(T_1T_2B_1B_2) = P(T_1T_2)P(B_1)P(B_2)$
  • $P(T_1B_1B_2) = P(T_1)P(B_1)P(B_2)$
  • $P(T_2B_1B_2) = P(T_2)P(B_1)P(B_2)$

Theorem

$P(B_1B_2\ |\ B_1T_1 + B_2T_2) = \dfrac{2bP(T_1) - bP(T_1T_2)}{2P(T_1) - bP(T_1T_2)}$

Case: $T$ = child is the younger than the other one

Cannot be true for both children at the same time: $P(T_1T_2) = 0$. Equation reduces to $b = 1/2$.

Case: $T$ is empty

For $P(T_1) = 1$, and $P(T_1T_2) = 1$ this equation reduces to $b/(2-b) = 1/3$.

Case: $T$ = child is born on a tuesday

$t = 1/7$, $P(T_1 T_2) = P(T_1)P(T_2) = 1/49$. The equation reduces to $13/27$.
